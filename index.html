Hello, I'm Bradley, an undergrad student at the Georgia Institute of Technology studying mathematics, algorithms, and quantum computing.

who I am

I'm in love with all the different ways people solve problems—I've been learning about math, physics, and computer science for as long as I can remember, and I hope to make a career out of solving tough technical problems and finding crazy insights.

Outside of all that, I try to get outside (to stay sane) as much as I can. I enjoy cooking, weightlifting, and making music. On campus, I play with Georgia Tech's jazz ensemble, and swim with our swim club —and best of all, I run intramural sports teams for every sport, every season. I'm also an avid pickleballer, and sometimes I like to go find parks and hikes around Atlanta.

what I'm doing

Along with taking classes (which are listed here), I'm doing research with the CRNCH Lab at Georgia Tech's School of Computer Science. There, I help out with implementing important quantum algorithms in a new under-development programming language for quantum computers.

In 2023 I worked with the particle theory group at Kennesaw State University. There, I was immersed in quantum field theory within and beyond the Standard Model. My work made predictions for a possible experiment at a collider based on a model for an undiscovered particle, the Z' boson.
